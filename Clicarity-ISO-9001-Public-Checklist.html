Ask [CEO Name] to sit with you · Go through the risks together
Read each risk below out loud. [CEO Name] tells you the L number and the I number. You write them in the table. Then multiply them together to get the Score.
📋 Risk scoring — what the numbers mean
  • L = Likelihood — how likely is this to happen? 1 = very unlikely, 5 = very likely
  • I = Impact — how bad is it if it happens? 1 = tiny problem, 5 = huge problem
  • Score = L × I — if Score is 15 or more, it is a HIGH risk and needs a control written in the last column
📋 Read these risks to [CEO Name] one by one
  • Customer order not delivered on time
  • Product or service quality failure causing customer complaint
  • Key employee leaving — process knowledge lost
  • Main supplier failing to deliver materials or services
  • Customer data going to wrong person by accident
  • Equipment or system breakdown stopping work
  • Legal or government regulation changing unexpectedly
  • New product or service launch causing problems with existing work
  • Customer complaint not resolved in time
  • Cash flow problem affecting ability to deliver

Understand what an SOP is before you start
SOP = Standard Operating Procedure. It is a simple 1-page document that explains how [Company Name] does something. You write it in numbered steps. Use simple words. Write what actually happens — not what you wish happened.
