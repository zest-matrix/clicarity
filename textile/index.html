Your Production Flow

Every batch. Every dye lot. Every metre accounted for.

This is how a print job moves through your business. Clicarity gives you live visibility at every phase — and flags delays before they become client calls.

01
Planning & Procurement
4 stages
Order / Production Plan
Buyer order or internal production plan confirmed — fabric type, count, colour, quantity.
✓ Clicarity: Batch ID created, dye lot assigned, production initiated
Yarn Procurement
Yarn procured from spinner or pulled from inventory — count, composition, shade.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: yarn lot variation — shade inconsistency discovered mid-production
Dye & Chemical Procurement
Dyes, chemicals, and auxiliaries procured per recipe for the batch.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: dye stock-out halts batch mid-process
Incoming Inspection
Yarn tested for count, strength, and shade before entering production.
✓ Clicarity: Lot number and supplier recorded — full traceability chain begins
02
Yarn Preparation & Weaving / Knitting
5 stages
Winding / Warping
Yarn wound onto cones or warped onto beams per production requirement.
✓ Clicarity: Yarn quantity in/out tracked — first loss checkpoint
Sizing
Warp yarn sized with starch or chemical for weaving strength.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: sizing recipe variation — warp breakages on loom increase
Weaving / Knitting
Fabric woven on looms or knitted on machines to required construction.
✓ Clicarity: Loom-wise production tracked — idle looms flagged automatically
Grey Fabric Inspection
Woven or knitted grey fabric inspected for defects before wet processing.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: high defect rate discovered after entire batch woven
Batching
Inspected grey fabric batched for wet processing — lot numbers assigned.
✓ Clicarity: Batch quantity confirmed, dye lot matched to batch
03
Wet Processing
6 stages
Scouring / Desizing
Fabric scoured to remove sizing and impurities before dyeing.
Bleaching
Fabric bleached to required whiteness for dyeing or as finished product.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: bleaching variation — uneven base causes dye rejection
Dyeing
Fabric dyed to required shade in jet, jigger, or continuous dyeing range.
✓ Clicarity: Dye lot tracked, shade approval status logged before proceeding
Washing
Dyed fabric washed to remove unfixed dye and chemicals.
Softening / Chemical Finishing
Softener or functional finish applied as per buyer specification.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: finish recipe not communicated correctly — batch reworked
Drying
Fabric dried to required moisture content before mechanical finishing.
✓ Clicarity: Quantity in vs out tracked — shrinkage recorded at this stage
04
Mechanical Finishing
4 stages
Stentering / Heat Setting
Fabric set to required width and dimensional stability on stenter.
✓ Clicarity: Width and metre count logged — shrinkage vs plan visible live
Raising / Brushing
Surface finish raised or brushed for fleece or brushed fabric types.
Calendering / Compacting
Fabric passed through calender for smoothness or compacted for stability.
⚠ ⚠ Common delay: machine settings off — batch needs rework before dispatch
Final Inspection
Fabric inspected on inspection table — defects marked, grade assigned.
✓ Clicarity: Grade-wise quantity logged — A/B/C grade split visible per batch
05
Dispatch
3 stages
Folding / Rolling / Packing
Fabric folded or rolled and packed as per buyer requirement.
✓ Clicarity: Packed metre count vs order quantity confirmed before dispatch
Dispatch
Consignment dispatched with shade card, test reports, and delivery challan.
✓ Clicarity: Dispatch timestamp and lot details logged. Batch closed.
Buyer Confirmation
Buyer acknowledges receipt and shade approval.
✓ Clicarity: Full batch history from yarn to dispatch preserved
Where Textile Manufacturers Lose Fabric & Margin

Real bottlenecks. Real costs. Real fixes.

These are the patterns Clicarity helps textile businesses identify and fix within the first few weeks.

Dyeing Stage
22% of batches failing shade approval — cause unknown for months
A textile mill tracked shade approval outcomes against dye lot numbers and machine used. 22% of batches were failing first-pass shade approval — requiring rework dyeing. Clicarity showed the failures were concentrated on one specific jet dyeing machine running at wrong liquor ratio. Machine recalibrated on day 8. First-pass approval rate improved to 94%.
Dye rejection reduced from 22% to 6% — machine fault identified
Shrinkage Loss
Finishing shrinkage running 8% above standard — invisible until dispatch
A fabric manufacturer tracked metre-in vs metre-out at the stentering stage. Shrinkage was consistently 8% above the planned 3% — meaning every 1,000 metre batch was yielding 890 metres instead of 970. Over a month, the loss was 24,000 metres. Stenter temperature profile was wrong. Corrected in week 2. Monthly metre loss reduced by 18,000 metres.
18,000 metres per month saved — stenter setting corrected
Loom Downtime
Six looms idle every afternoon — supervisor assumed they were running
A weaving unit tracked production output per loom per shift. Six out of 24 looms were consistently showing zero output in the afternoon shift — but the supervisor's daily report showed "all looms running." With live visibility, idle looms flagged automatically. Investigation revealed weavers were taking extended breaks without stopping the counter. Output per shift increased 22%.
Loom output per shift increased 22% — idle time made visible

Questions from textile businesses

How does Clicarity work for a printing business specifically?
Clicarity is configured around your specific print workflow — pre-press, printing, finishing, dispatch — in your terminology. Every job gets a unique ID and moves through your defined stages. You see live status, timestamps, and bottleneck alerts at every stage. The Clicarity team builds everything for you. You go live in 5 days.
Does it work for both offset and digital printing?
Yes. Clicarity works for any print process — offset, digital, wide format, screen printing, or a combination. The stages are configured around your actual workflow. If offset jobs go through plate making and digital jobs skip that stage, both can be tracked correctly under the same system.
Can it track material wastage at each stage?
Yes — with the Multi Licence. At every stage, your team logs quantity in and quantity out. Clicarity calculates the loss automatically. You see wastage by stage, by job, live — not at month-end when nothing can be done about it.
Will my press operators actually use it?
Yes. The update screen has two fields — Job ID and status. Under a minute. No training needed. GP Offset's entire team was using it from day one, including floor workers who had never used a software system before.
