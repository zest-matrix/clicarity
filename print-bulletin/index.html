The full founder story

Unlike a standard product feature, Print Bulletin's Learning Curve format told the complete narrative — the problem, the insight, the solution, and the real businesses using it.

🏭
Where it started
Puneet Rawat — Mumbai-based printpreneur — saw the same chaos across nearly two decades in printing and packaging. Production updates via phone calls, handwritten notes, verbal confirmations. Every factory had the same broken system.
💡
The insight
The problem was never the people. It was the absence of a simple, visible system. When there's no visibility, blame circulates. Everyone waits. No one knows. The article named this the "standardisation of excuses."
📱
The solution
Two screens. Create Job. Update Status. If someone can use WhatsApp, they can use Clicarity. No training required. Live in days. Built around the client's own job docket — their format, their stages, their language.
What made this article unforgettable
The feature used the exact words every shop floor manager in India has heard — written as illustration captions in the magazine.
"Client ko kya bolun?"
What do I tell the client? — The daily anxiety of not knowing where a job actually is.
"Sir, kal tak ho jayega"
Sir, it'll be done by tomorrow — the verbal promise with no system behind it.
"Humein toh material late mila"
We got the material late — the blame that circulates when there's no production trail.

What is Print Bulletin — and why this placement matters

Print Bulletin is an initiative by BMPA (Bombay Master Printers' Association) and MMS — one of Mumbai's most respected trade associations for the print and packaging industry.

The Learning Curve section is reserved for stories the editorial team believes the industry should learn from. This isn't an ad — it's an editorial endorsement.
