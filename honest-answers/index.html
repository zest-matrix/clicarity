About adoption & team
My workers are not tech-savvy. They won't use it.
▾
This is the most common concern we hear — and the one that disappears fastest after going live. Clicarity has exactly two screens: Create Job and Update Status. The Update screen shows only the fields relevant to that operator's stage. Nothing else.

If your operator can use WhatsApp — and most of them do — they can use Clicarity. We've seen 60-year-old press operators in Mumbai adopt it within a day. The system is designed around the way they already think about their job, not around software logic.
💡 We set up the system to mirror your existing job docket. Operators see their own stages, their own language, their own process.
My supervisor will feel threatened or bypassed.
▾
Understandable concern — and an important one. The honest answer: supervisors who are doing their job well have nothing to fear. Clicarity doesn't replace the supervisor. It removes the part of their job that was always frustrating — being the middleman for information that should flow automatically.

Supervisors actually gain with Clicarity. They stop getting called every 30 minutes for updates they don't have. Their team's work becomes visible — which means their own contribution becomes visible too. The ones who resist most before going live are often the most vocal advocates after.
We tried software before and it was a disaster.
▾
That's the most important thing you can tell us. It means you know exactly what went wrong — and we can make sure it doesn't happen again.

Most failed implementations we've heard about share three causes: the software required the factory to change its process to fit the software; implementation took months and momentum died; the interface was too complex for shop floor operators.

Clicarity does the opposite on all three. It mirrors your existing process. It goes live in days, not months. And it's designed specifically for operators who've never used software before.

We'll show you exactly how in a free demo — no pressure.
We don't have time to implement new software right now.
▾
This is actually the most honest reason — and we respect it. But consider this: the busier your factory is, the more you're losing to the problems Clicarity solves. Delays, rework, vendor confusion, morning follow-ups — these multiply with volume.

Implementation takes 3–7 days. Not months. We configure the system, train the team in one session, and you're tracking live. The time cost of going live is less than one day's worth of follow-up calls.

About the product fit
We already use WhatsApp for updates. Why change?
▾
WhatsApp is brilliant for conversation. It's terrible for tracking. Can you tell me right now — without scrolling through three groups — exactly where Job #1041 is, who updated it last, and when it was moved to lamination?

Clicarity doesn't replace WhatsApp. Your team still uses WhatsApp for everything else. Vendors still receive updates via WhatsApp. But job status lives in Clicarity — searchable, timestamped, visible to everyone who needs to see it, automatically.

We're too small for this kind of software.
▾
The smallest factory we work with has 5 people. The problem Clicarity solves — not knowing where jobs are without calling someone — exists at 5 people just as much as at 500. In fact, smaller factories often see results faster because the system is simpler to configure and everyone adopts it quickly.

There's no minimum factory size. If you have jobs, stages, and at least two people — Clicarity works for you.
Our process is too unique — no software can fit it.
▾
We hear this often — and in our experience, it's very rarely true. Every factory's product is unique. But the underlying pattern is always the same: a job moves through stages, people update progress, an owner needs to see where things are.

Clicarity is built to mirror your existing job docket. When you sign up, we convert your physical job card or Excel format into the system. Your stages, your fields, your language. If you use "Curing" instead of "QC" — that's what appears in Clicarity.
